Before You Begin

  1. Review eligibility guidelines
  2. Gather required documents

You'll need to upload one document from each category:

  • Proof of age
    • Birth certificate
    • Photo ID (driver's license, state ID, learner's permit, passport, school ID, etc.)
    • Visa, residency, or immigration document
    • A notarized affidavit from a parent or legal guardian
  • Proof of address
    • Photo ID (driver's license, state ID, learner's permit, etc.)
    • Utility or phone bill
    • Signed lease agreement
    • Bank statement, W2, or pay stub
    • Official school transcript, proof of enrollment, or tuition bill for current year
  • Proof of enrollment in a partner program, if you are 18 or older
    • Program card (MassHealth, SNAP, WIC, etc.)
    • Enrollment letter (Head Start, Fuel Assistance, Year Up, Roca, etc.)
    • Screenshot from DTA Connect app or website (SNAP, EAEDC, etc.)
    • Other document (Housing Authority lease, Section 8/MRVP voucher, financial aid award letter through MASSGrant, etc.)

Contact your school

Many middle schools and high schools offer reduced fare Student CharlieCards through the MBTA's Student CharlieCard Program. Homeschooled students can request a Student CharlieCard by completing an order form.

Contact your school directly to see if you can get a Student CharlieCard (M7 or S-Card). If you cannot get a Student CharlieCard through your school, you may return to this application and apply for a Youth Pass.

Enrollment in a partner program is required

You must be enrolled in an MBTA-approved program to qualify for a Youth Pass. Learn more about partner programs.

Youth Pass partners with many state and federal, education, and job training programs. Check our list of partner programs and required documents to see if you qualify.
